How Spatial Data Predicts Post-Transfer Success

By Dr. Lara F. Singh (Head of Data) October 15, 2025 Analytics

At Forum Sports Management, our core belief is that the most successful career moves are not based on intuition, but on quantitative validation. This is why we have integrated cutting-edge geospatial analytics—the science of location data—into our advisory process.

Traditional scouting relies on match footage and basic stats. Our system goes deeper, using **Geographic Information Systems (GIS)**, similar to those used in urban planning and risk modeling, to overlay a player's performance data with critical environmental factors. This includes **pitch density mapping**, optimal travel logistics, and even the "spatial fit" with a club's defensive or offensive zones.

Leveraging GIS for Optimal Fit

For a defensive midfielder, for example, we track the spatial distribution of their successful tackles and interceptions. By overlaying this **geospatial data** onto the tactical formations of potential new clubs, we can generate a predictive metric (an "Expected Success Rate") for that player in a new environment. This significantly de-risks transfers for both the player and the club. We leverage proficiency in languages like **Python and R** to handle the large datasets necessary for this modeling.

Our commitment to rigorous quantitative data analysis, honed by experience in fields like financial and project management, ensures our advice is scientifically robust. When we recommend a move, it's because our models, validated by a portfolio of practical exercise results, predict success.
